Understanding Color Formats

HEX Colors

Hexadecimal color codes are widely used in web design. They consist of 6 characters (0-9, A-F) representing red, green, and blue values. Example: #4CAF50

RGB Colors

RGB (Red, Green, Blue) uses three numbers from 0-255 to define colors. This format is commonly used in CSS and programming. Example: rgb(76, 175, 80)

HSL Colors

HSL (Hue, Saturation, Lightness) is intuitive for adjusting colors. Hue is 0-360 degrees, while saturation and lightness are percentages. Example: hsl(122, 39%, 49%)

Color Theory Tips

Complementary Colors

Colors opposite on the color wheel create high contrast and vibrant looks. Great for call-to-action buttons.

Analogous Colors

Colors next to each other on the wheel create harmonious, pleasing designs. Perfect for backgrounds and gradients.

Accessibility

Ensure sufficient contrast between text and background (minimum 4.5:1 ratio for normal text). Use tools to check WCAG compliance.
